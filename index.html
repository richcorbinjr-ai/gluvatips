Pilot Education

Small moves, steadier glucose

A few simple habits can flatten the spike your body gets after a meal. Skim these, keep one or two in mind, and put them to work during your next round of CGM readings.

⏱ 30-second read · pick your favorites
Lowest-Hanging Fruit

The quick wins

Ranked easiest-first. You don't need all of them — even one or two, done regularly, will show up in your glucose data.

1

Walk for 10 minutes after you eat

The single most powerful thing you can do. A short walk after a meal lets your muscles soak up glucose instead of letting it spike.

↓ post-meal spike up to 30%
2

Eat your food in the right order

Veggies and protein first, carbs and sugar last. Same meal, smaller spike — just by changing the order you eat it.

easy · works at any meal
3

Do 10 air squats or calf raises

No gym needed. A minute of moving your biggest muscles — even calf raises on a call — pulls sugar out of your blood.

60 seconds · anywhere
4

Don't drink your sugar

Soda, juice, and sweet coffee drinks spike glucose fastest of all. Swap one a day for water or unsweetened — an easy win.

biggest spikes come in liquid form
5

Add protein or fat to a carb

Never eat a carb naked. Pair toast with eggs, fruit with nuts, crackers with cheese — it slows the sugar down.

flattens the curve
6

Keep meals off the couch

Staying completely still right after eating is when spikes run highest. Tidy up, stand, or stretch instead of sitting down.

light activity beats stillness
7

Get your sleep

One bad night of sleep makes your body handle sugar worse the next day. Steady sleep is quietly one of the biggest levers.

poor sleep = higher next-day glucose
8

Watch your CGM after new foods

Your monitor is a teacher. When you try a food, glance at your response — you'll quickly learn what spikes you personally.

your data is personal
If you remember just one thing: move a little after every meal.
Move a little — spike a lot less.
